# Product

## Users

People across Brazil who enjoy online poker with friends or public opponents. They do not need to be existing CTech
community members. The primary context is social play: gathering around a dependable online table from any
internet-connected device, then competing for money and progressing through rankings and achievements.

## Product Purpose

CTech Poker is a responsive online Texas Hold'em game built to feel alive rather than static. It exists to make playing
with friends effortless while delivering smooth, low-latency action, public matchmaking, private rooms, real-money and
sandbox play, and visible progression. A successful session begins with social connection, supports the excitement of
winning money, and gives returning players meaningful progress to pursue.

## Positioning

The vivid, low-latency poker table for playing with friends, where every hand is completely random, fair, and auditable.

## Brand Personality

Vivid, trustworthy, social. The game should carry the lively, responsive energy of Mega Hit Poker: Texas Holdem into a
device-independent web experience. It feels animated and present without becoming noisy; competitive without becoming
predatory; and friendly without becoming childish.

## Anti-references

- PokerStars-style static or frozen-feeling play, where cards, players, and state changes lack presence.
- Casino clutter: dense promotions, flashing chrome, competing calls to action, and visual noise around the table.
- Predatory gambling patterns, including urgency pressure, loss-chasing prompts, manipulative rewards, or obscured money
  semantics.
- Childish gamification that weakens trust in real-money play.
- Crypto aesthetics: neon speculation, token hype, terminal styling, and volatility theater.

## Design Principles

1. **Make the table feel alive.** Cards, chips, turns, reactions, and player presence should move smoothly and
   communicate state without delaying play.
2. **Friends before features.** Joining, inviting, recognizing, and enjoying other players is the heart of the
   experience; competition and progression build on that social foundation.
3. **Fairness must be inspectable.** Randomness, hand outcomes, money state, and auditability should be explained in
   plain language and surfaced where players need reassurance.
4. **Speed is part of trust.** Input, dealing, betting, and reconnecting must feel immediate. Loading, latency, and
   recovery states should be visible and honest rather than leaving the table frozen.
5. **One CTech family, a distinct game.** Preserve the CTech products' clarity, restrained component vocabulary,
   explicit state semantics, Geist typography, and trust-first behavior while giving Poker its own vivid, dark,
   table-focused identity.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.2 AA is the baseline. The complete game must support keyboard operation, visible focus, reduced-motion
alternatives, readable contrast, and responsive layouts from mobile web through desktop. Suits, actions, player state,
wins, errors, and turn ownership must never rely on color alone; use text, shape, iconography, and position so
color-blind players receive the same information. Copy is pt-BR-first and must remain ready for localization without
truncation or overflow.
